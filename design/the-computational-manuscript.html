The Computational Manuscript

a design language for living documents
MMXXVI

his document describes a design language that holds three tensions simultaneously: sacred and technical, precise and human, still and alive. It emerged from the construction of thirty-six animated geometric plates, a prose treatise on self-similarity, and a shared infrastructure of ornamental artefacts. The language is called the computational manuscript — code given the hierarchy, gravity, and page-furniture of an illuminated treatise. Every principle it describes, it performs. The page you are reading is the design language.

§1 The Three Tensions

Tension I — Sacred & Technical
The content is mathematical, algorithmic, geometric — but the presentation carries the weight of an illuminated manuscript. A Lorenz attractor is rendered with the same care as a chapter of scripture. The page treats its subjects with devotion.
Tension II — Precise & Human
Every curve is computed, but every surface feels written. A mulberry32 PRNG seeds authored imperfection: corner radii vary ±0.8px, stroke widths drift ±0.15, control points wobble ±1–5px. The geometry was plotted, not drawn by CAD.
Tension III — Still & Alive
Motion is the medium, not a polish layer. Orrery rings rotate once per 55 seconds. Pendulums swing at 8–20 second periods. The code ghost scrolls. Headpieces redraw themselves. Without continuous motion, the aesthetic collapses into a static costume.

§2 The Paper

The page is not white. It is paper — warm, uneven, grained. Three systems: a layered radial gradient (exposure at upper-left 30% 8%, fill at lower-right 70% 92%), an SVG feTurbulence grain (fractalNoise, 0.65 freq, 6 octaves, 18% opacity, mix-blend-mode: multiply), and a radial vignette (transparent center to 8% black at edges). The folio floats on a dark surround — a manuscript on a scholar's desk.

§5 The Motion

Five categories: astronomical rotation (40–140s, linear), the breath (6–18s, ease-in-out, unique phase per element), the trace (rAF following a computed curve), self-drawing paths (stroke-dashoffset 1.2–2.6s), and the redraw (headpieces cycling every 30s). Plus the ghost scroll — the code that builds the view, scrolling at 180s per loop behind the content.
